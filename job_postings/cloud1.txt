Position: Cloud Engineer
Company: Cardinal Health
Location: Taguig
Pay: N/A

Job Description:
Cardinal Health, a global leader in healthcare innovation, is seeking a Cloud Engineer 
to help drive the evolution of our enterprise‑scale cloud and CI/CD platforms. 
Operating in a multi‑cloud, multi‑tenant environment, our platforms support thousands of developers across 
hundreds of teams, powering mission‑critical workloads across IaaS, PaaS, and CaaS models.

As part of the Serverless Platforms Team, you will help design, build, operate, and continuously 
improve our cloud‑native and CI/CD platforms—including Kubernetes, serverless runtimes, and the 
enterprise Harness CI/CD ecosystem. You will work closely with application teams, security partners, and 
platform stakeholders to deliver secure, scalable, and repeatable deployment patterns using GitOps, infrastructure as code, and modern DevSecOps practices.

With a strong foundation in cloud‑native development and platform engineering, 
you will help ensure our platforms are reliable, governed, and easy to consume—while fostering 
a culture of technical excellence, automation, and continuous improvement. If you are passionate 
about Kubernetes, CI/CD platforms, and solving complex platform‑scale challenges, join us to help shape the future of healthcare technology.


Role Summary:
As a Cloud Engineer, your primary responsibility will be to design, deploy, and support containerized and serverless platforms on Google Kubernetes Engine 
(GKE) and to operate and evolve the enterprise Harness CI/CD platform. You will enable application teams to build, test, and deploy software through standardized, 
secure, and scalable pipelines while ensuring platform reliability, compliance, and developer experience.


Responsibilities:
- Design, deploy, and manage Kubernetes clusters and workloads on GKE using Infrastructure as Code and GitOps practices.
- Operate and support the enterprise Harness CI/CD platform, including build, deploy, and infrastructure pipelines, ensuring availability, reliability, and consistent developer experience across teams.
- Develop and maintain standardized Harness pipeline templates for build, test, security scanning, and deployment to Kubernetes and serverless runtimes.
- Partner with application teams to onboard services to Harness, providing guidance, troubleshooting, and best‑practice enablement.
- Integrate CI/CD pipelines with Infrastructure as Code tooling (e.g., Terraform) and GitOps solutions (e.g., ArgoCD, Flux) to support repeatable and auditable deployments.
- Embed security and compliance controls into CI/CD workflows, including vulnerability scanning, policy enforcement, secrets management, and audit logging to meet HIPAA, SOX, and enterprise security requirements.
- Collaborate with DevSecOps and Application Security teams to shift security left within the SDLC, leveraging tools such as Trivy, Aqua, or equivalent scanners integrated into Harness pipelines.
- Monitor platform health, pipeline execution, and application performance using tools such as Dynatrace, Prometheus, and cloud‑native monitoring; perform root cause analysis and drive incident resolution.
- Participate in code and pipeline reviews, establish platform standards, and ensure adherence to engineering best practices.
- Enforce platform governance and guardrails using policy‑as‑code approaches (e.g., OPA), ensuring consistency, observability, and compliance across all environments.
- Act as a technical liaison between the Serverless Platforms Team and partner organizations, translating business and delivery needs into scalable platform capabilities.
- Communicate platform changes, incident status, and best practices through clear runbooks, release notes, and stakeholder updates; facilitate working sessions to drive alignment and unblock delivery.
- Stay current with cloud‑native and CI/CD trends, contributing to the ongoing evolution of Cardinal Health’s cloud and DevOps strategy.


Qualifications:
- Bachelor’s degree in Computer Science, Engineering, or equivalent practical experience.
- 3+ years of experience in cloud engineering with a strong focus on Kubernetes and GCP (preferred).
- Hands‑on experience designing, operating, or supporting CI/CD platforms, preferably Harness CI/CD in an enterprise environment.
- Strong understanding of microservices, event‑driven architectures, and distributed systems.
- Experience with GitOps, Infrastructure as Code, and automated delivery pipelines.
- Familiarity with DevSecOps practices, security scanning, and compliance requirements in regulated environments.
- Exceptional communication skills—able to translate complex platform and security concepts for both technical and non‑technical audiences, and to influence outcomes through clear documentation and stakeholder alignment.
- Proven problem‑solving skills and the ability to operate effectively in complex, evolving environments.
